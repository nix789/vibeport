# Vibeport — Terms of Service
## "The Declaration of Independence"

*Last updated: 2026-03-14. This is a living document. Changes are proposed via
public commit on the Vibeport protocol repository — never by unilateral decree.*

---

### Article 1 — Zero Jurisdiction

We cannot ban you because we do not host you.

Your Port is your sovereign territory. Vibeport is a protocol — a set of open
rules for how nodes communicate — not a company with servers, employees, or a
terms-enforcement team. There is no central authority capable of deplatforming
you, throttling your reach, or reading your messages.

You are the sole distributor of your content. You are therefore solely
responsible for what you publish. If your content violates the laws of your
jurisdiction, the legal exposure is yours — not ours, because "ours" does not
exist as a legal entity capable of receiving liability.

---

### Article 2 — No Platform Fees. Ever.

Vibeport is a protocol, not a company.

We will never take a percentage of your tips, micropayments, or "insider
payouts." Value flows directly from Peer A to Peer B via the Lightning Network
(or any payment rail you choose to implement at your node). There is no payment
processor, no escrow, no rake.

If someone forks this protocol and introduces fees, they are not Vibeport.
The spec is open. The reference implementation is open. Any fork that adds
a revenue mechanism is a different product.

---

### Article 3 — The Little Guy Clause

This network is architecturally hostile to whale dominance.

There are no "Boosts," no paid reach, no promoted posts, and no advertising API.
The protocol has no mechanism to purchase visibility. Resonance is earned through
direct human interaction — a friend sharing your node key, a sticker sent, a
webring invitation. These interactions cost nothing except attention, which
cannot be bought at scale without becoming obvious.

Nodes with large follower counts have no technical advantage over nodes with one.
Every Port announces itself to the DHT on equal footing.

---

### Article 4 — What We Ask of You

Since we cannot enforce anything, this is a request, not a rule:

- Do not use this protocol to coordinate real-world violence.
- Do not use it to distribute content that exploits children.
- Do not use it to impersonate another person's keypair identity.

The network cannot stop you from doing any of these things. Your neighbors on
the DHT can choose not to replicate your data. That is the enforcement
mechanism: social ostracism, not corporate moderation.

---

*By using Vibeport, you agree that you have read, understood, and accepted full
personal responsibility for your activity on this network. There is no company
to sue, no support ticket to file, and no appeals process. There is only the
network and your neighbors on it.*
